Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to the most common questions from netGolfLeague administrators and members.

Handicaps
NGL uses a 1–9 scale to accommodate the wide variety of courses leagues play on. Some courses have 36 holes, some have just 9, so a standardized 1–9 handicap works consistently across all configurations.
First-week scorecard entries must include a handicap value; otherwise golfers default to a 0 handicap (scratch). A simple approach: take each golfer's Week 1 score minus par and use that as their seed handicap. This prevents everyone from starting at scratch.
Two options exist: use all season scores across all years (the default), or restrict calculations to the current season only. Configure this in Setup → League and Season Options → Handicap Calculation.
The NCGA (and similar regional associations) rate courses every six years using trained volunteer raters who evaluate 10 obstacles per hole. Official ratings are available in the USGA course database.
USGA maximums are 18.2 for men and 20.2 for women. However, the resulting Course Handicap can be higher on courses with slope ratings exceeding 113, since the slope multiplier increases the number.
This almost always means handicaps weren't entered before Week 1 scores were posted, so golfers defaulted to scratch (0). Always enter seed handicaps before posting the first round. The recommended seed: each golfer's score minus par.
Scoring
Stableford is a points-based scoring format where you earn points on each hole based on your score relative to par (adjusted for handicap). Bogey = 1 point, Par = 2, Birdie = 3, Eagle = 4, etc. The golfer with the most total points wins — rather than the fewest strokes. It rewards aggressive play and is forgiving of one bad hole.
Select "Match Play – Wins/Losses" or "Match Play – Total Points" from the Team Scoring System dropdown in Setup. Then customize the scoring parameters via Team Scoring Configuration to match your league's format.
Pairings designate which golfers are active for each match week and enable team substitutions across multiple players. Without pairings, the system doesn't know who's playing that week, so names can't populate the scorecard entry screen.
Choose "Best Ball – Points" or "Best Ball – Wins/Losses" as your team scoring system. Then configure the scoring parameters (points per hole, match points, etc.) in Team Scoring Configuration. In Best Ball / Fourball, each team uses the lowest score from its members on each hole.
Golfer Management
In Golfer Management, select the golfer names you want to contact, choose Invite from the Email dropdown, then click the Email button. This sends a batch email to each selected golfer with their personal login credentials.
Use Quick Add Golfer and enter their email address. If that address already exists in the NGL system, their account is automatically linked to your league — no duplicate profile is created and their login stays the same.
Click Edit on the Golfers section, then click the Add button. Fill in the required fields (marked with asterisks). Email is not mandatory — you can create a golfer record without one.
